Overview

What Is Teeth Scaling?

🦠

Plaque Removal

Plaque is a sticky bacterial film that constantly builds up on your teeth. Scaling uses ultrasonic and hand instruments to thoroughly remove it above and below the gum line.

🪨

Tartar (Calculus) Removal

When plaque hardens it becomes tartar — a stubborn deposit that cannot be removed by brushing alone. Professional scaling is the only way to safely eliminate it.

✨

Polishing

After scaling, teeth are polished to remove surface stains and create a smooth finish that makes it harder for plaque to re-attach quickly.

🛡️

Gum Disease Prevention

Regular scaling keeps bacteria levels low, preventing gingivitis from progressing into periodontitis — a serious condition that can lead to tooth loss.

Candidacy

Who Needs Teeth Scaling?

🩸

Bleeding Gums

If your gums bleed while brushing or flossing, it is an early sign of gingivitis caused by plaque buildup — scaling treats this effectively.

💨

Bad Breath (Halitosis)

Persistent bad breath is often caused by bacteria hiding in tartar deposits. Scaling removes the source of odour for fresher breath.

🟡

Yellow or Stained Teeth

Surface stains from tea, coffee, or tobacco that have bonded to tartar deposits are removed during professional scaling and polishing.

😬

Swollen or Sensitive Gums

Inflamed, red, or tender gums indicate bacterial irritation. Scaling clears the deposits that cause this inflammation.

📅

Routine Maintenance

Even with perfect brushing habits, professional scaling every 6 months is recommended to remove deposits that form in hard-to-reach areas.

🦷

Before Other Dental Treatments

Scaling is often performed before braces, crowns, or implant procedures to ensure the mouth is in the healthiest possible condition.

Why Choose Scaling

Benefits of Professional Teeth Scaling

💪

Stronger, Healthier Gums

Removing tartar and bacteria halts gum inflammation, restoring gum tissue to a firm, pink, healthy state.

🛡️

Prevents Tooth Loss

Untreated gum disease is the leading cause of adult tooth loss. Regular scaling stops the disease before it destroys supporting bone.

😁

Brighter Smile

Polishing after scaling removes surface stains, leaving teeth visibly cleaner and brighter — with no whitening chemicals needed.

🌿

Fresh Breath

Eliminating the bacterial deposits that cause odour gives you lasting fresh breath and greater confidence in daily interactions.

❤️

Improves Overall Health

Gum disease is linked to heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. Keeping your gums clean through scaling supports your whole-body health.

⚡

Quick & Painless

Most scaling sessions are completed in 30–60 minutes with minimal discomfort. No injections or surgery required in routine cases.

Step by Step

The Scaling Process at MCR

1

Oral Examination

Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy performs a thorough examination of your teeth and gums to assess the level of tartar buildup and check for signs of gum disease.

2

Ultrasonic Scaling

An ultrasonic scaler uses high-frequency vibrations with a water spray to break apart and flush away large tartar deposits quickly and comfortably.

3

Hand Scaling

Fine hand instruments are used to carefully remove remaining deposits, especially those in deep pockets around the gum line that need precise attention.

4

Root Planing (If Needed)

If gum disease is present, the root surfaces are smoothed (planed) to remove bacterial toxins and allow the gum tissue to reattach properly to the tooth.

5

Polishing

A rotating brush with a mild polishing paste is used to buff the tooth surfaces, removing residual stains and leaving teeth smooth and clean.

6

Aftercare Advice

Your dentist provides personalised brushing and flossing instructions and schedules your next cleaning appointment to maintain your results.

Care & Safety

Aftercare & What to Expect

🥶

Mild Sensitivity

Some teeth and gum sensitivity for 1–3 days after scaling is normal. Avoid very hot, cold, or sweet foods during this period. A sensitivity toothpaste helps.

🥣

Soft Diet Initially

Stick to soft, non-spicy foods for the first 24–48 hours. Avoid hard, crunchy foods that could irritate tender gum tissue while it settles.

🪥

Maintain Oral Hygiene

Brush twice daily with a soft-bristle brush and floss regularly. Using an antibacterial mouthwash as advised helps keep bacteria at bay.

📅

Regular Follow-ups

Schedule professional scaling every 6 months to prevent tartar from rebuilding. Patients with gum disease may need more frequent visits initially.

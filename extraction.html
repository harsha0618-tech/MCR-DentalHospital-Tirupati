Overview

What Is Tooth Extraction?

🦷

Simple Extraction

A simple extraction is performed on a tooth that is visible in the mouth. After applying local anaesthesia to numb the area completely, Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy gently loosens and removes the tooth using specialised instruments — quickly and with minimal discomfort.

⚕️

Surgical Extraction

Surgical extraction is required for teeth that are broken at the gum line, impacted, or haven't fully erupted — such as wisdom teeth. A small incision is made to access the tooth, which is then carefully removed in sections if necessary to minimise trauma to surrounding tissue.

💉

Comfortable Anaesthesia

We use modern local anaesthetic techniques to ensure the extraction site is completely numb before any procedure begins. You will feel pressure but no pain. Sedation options are also available for anxious patients to ensure a calm, stress-free experience.

🩹

Fast Recovery Support

Our team provides thorough aftercare instructions, prescribed medication where needed, and a follow-up review to monitor healing. Most patients return to normal activities within one to two days, with complete healing in a few weeks.

When Is Tooth Extraction Needed?

🦠

Severe Tooth Decay

When decay has destroyed so much of a tooth that a filling, crown, or root canal can no longer restore it, extraction is the safest option to eliminate infection and prevent it from spreading to neighbouring teeth and the jawbone.

💔

Cracked or Broken Tooth

A tooth that is fractured below the gum line or split in a way that makes restoration impossible must be extracted. Leaving a broken tooth untreated can lead to severe pain, abscess, and bone loss over time.

🌀

Impacted Wisdom Teeth

Wisdom teeth that are blocked from fully emerging can cause pain, swelling, crowding, and increased risk of infection. Surgical removal of impacted wisdom teeth prevents these complications and protects the alignment of existing teeth.

📐

Overcrowding Before Orthodontics

When there isn't enough space in the jaw for all teeth to align correctly, one or more teeth may need to be extracted before braces or aligner treatment. This creates the room needed for proper tooth movement and a balanced result.

🫁

Advanced Gum Disease

Severe periodontitis can loosen teeth by destroying the bone and tissue that support them. When a tooth can no longer be held securely in its socket despite treatment, extraction becomes necessary to protect the health of the surrounding structures.

👶

Retained Baby Teeth

If a primary (milk) tooth fails to fall out naturally and is blocking the eruption of the permanent tooth beneath it, it needs to be extracted to allow the adult tooth to emerge in the correct position without crowding or misalignment.

How It Works

Your Extraction Process at MCR

1

Initial Consultation & X-Ray

Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy examines your tooth, reviews your dental and medical history, and takes a digital X-ray to assess the root structure, bone condition, and the complexity of the extraction required.

2

Treatment Planning

Based on the assessment, a personalised extraction plan is prepared — including the type of extraction needed, anaesthesia approach, and any preparatory steps such as antibiotics for active infection.

3

Anaesthesia & Numbing

Local anaesthetic is administered around the tooth and surrounding gum tissue. We wait until the area is fully numb — typically within a few minutes — before proceeding to ensure you feel no pain throughout.

4

Extraction

For simple extractions, the tooth is gently loosened with a dental elevator and removed with forceps. For surgical cases, a small incision is made, and the tooth may be sectioned for easier, less traumatic removal.

5

Socket Care & Clot Formation

After the tooth is removed, the socket is cleaned and a gauze pad is placed to encourage a blood clot to form. In surgical cases, absorbable sutures may be placed to help the gum tissue close and heal correctly.

6

Aftercare & Follow-Up

You receive detailed recovery instructions, prescribed pain relief and antibiotics if needed, and a follow-up appointment to confirm healing. Replacement options are also discussed at this stage to plan your next steps.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

?

Does tooth extraction hurt?

The procedure itself is performed under local anaesthesia, so you should not feel any pain — only mild pressure or movement. Some soreness and swelling after the anaesthetic wears off is normal and manageable with prescribed medication.

?

How long does recovery take?

Most patients recover within 1–3 days for simple extractions. Surgical extractions, including wisdom tooth removal, may take 5–7 days for initial healing, with the socket fully closing over several weeks.

?

What can I eat after extraction?

Stick to soft foods — yoghurt, rice, dal, mashed vegetables, and soups — for the first 24–48 hours. Avoid hot, spicy, crunchy, or chewy foods, and do not use a straw, as suction can dislodge the clot and delay healing.

?

Do I need to replace the extracted tooth?

In most cases, yes — especially for permanent teeth. Leaving a gap can lead to neighbouring teeth drifting, bite problems, and bone loss. We will discuss implants, bridges, or dentures so you can make the best decision for your smile.

?

Is wisdom tooth removal different from a regular extraction?

Wisdom teeth are often impacted or partially erupted, requiring a surgical approach. The procedure takes slightly longer and recovery may involve more swelling, but with our experienced team and post-operative care, the process is well-managed and straightforward.

?

Can I go to work after a tooth extraction?

Most patients take the rest of the day off following the procedure. For simple extractions, many return to light work the next day. Physically demanding jobs may require 2–3 days of rest to avoid increased bleeding or discomfort.

Overview

What Are Braces & Aligners?

🔩

Metal Braces

The most widely used orthodontic option — stainless steel brackets bonded to teeth and connected by archwires that gradually guide teeth into proper alignment. Durable, effective, and budget-friendly.

🪨

Ceramic Braces

Tooth-coloured or clear ceramic brackets that blend with your natural smile. They work just like metal braces but are far less noticeable — ideal for appearance-conscious patients.

✨

Clear Aligners

A series of removable, virtually invisible plastic trays custom-made to your teeth. Each tray shifts teeth a little further, offering a discreet and comfortable alternative to traditional braces.

🛡️

Retainers

After active treatment, retainers hold teeth in their corrected positions, preventing relapse and ensuring your investment in a straight smile lasts a lifetime.

Candidacy

Who Needs Braces or Aligners?

😬

Crowded Teeth

When there is insufficient space in the jaw, teeth overlap or twist. Braces create space and guide each tooth into the correct position for a uniform smile.

↔️

Gaps Between Teeth

Gaps or spaces between teeth can affect appearance and trap food. Orthodontic treatment closes these spaces by moving teeth closer together gradually.

⬆️

Overbite or Underbite

If your upper teeth protrude too far forward or your lower teeth sit ahead of your upper teeth, braces can realign the jaws and teeth for a balanced bite.

↕️

Open Bite

When the upper and lower teeth do not meet when the mouth is closed, it can affect chewing and speech. Orthodontic treatment corrects this alignment issue effectively.

🔀

Crossbite

A crossbite occurs when some upper teeth sit inside the lower teeth. Left untreated, it causes uneven wear and jaw problems. Braces correct this bite discrepancy.

😁

Cosmetic Improvement

Even mildly crooked teeth that do not cause functional problems can be straightened for an improved smile, greater self-confidence, and better overall oral hygiene.

Why Choose Orthodontic Treatment

Benefits of Braces & Aligners

😁

A Straighter, Confident Smile

Properly aligned teeth dramatically enhance your appearance and give you the confidence to smile, speak, and socialise without hesitation.

🪥

Easier to Clean

Straight teeth are far easier to brush and floss effectively. Fewer tight, overlapping spaces means less plaque buildup and a significantly lower risk of cavities and gum disease.

🍽️

Better Chewing & Digestion

A corrected bite allows all teeth to meet evenly, improving chewing efficiency, reducing strain on individual teeth, and even aiding digestion.

🗣️

Improved Speech

Misaligned teeth and jaw discrepancies can cause lisps and speech difficulties. Orthodontic correction often leads to clearer, more confident speech.

🦷

Prevents Long-Term Damage

Correcting bite problems prevents abnormal wear on tooth surfaces, reduces jaw joint (TMJ) stress, and protects teeth from fractures caused by uneven bite forces.

⏳

Long-Lasting Results

With proper retainer use after treatment, the results of orthodontic therapy are permanent — giving you a lifetime of beautiful, well-aligned teeth.

Step by Step

The Braces Process at MCR

1

Initial Orthodontic Consultation

Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy performs a thorough examination of your teeth, jaw, and bite, and reviews X-rays to assess your specific alignment needs and treatment options.

2

Customised Treatment Plan

A personalised treatment plan is created, selecting the most suitable type of braces or aligners and estimating the treatment duration based on your case complexity.

3

Fitting of Braces or Aligners

Brackets are bonded to your teeth and connected with an archwire, or your first set of custom aligners is fitted. Mild pressure and slight discomfort for 2–3 days is completely normal.

4

Regular Adjustment Visits

Every 4–6 weeks, you visit the clinic for wire adjustments or to progress to the next aligner tray. Each adjustment continues moving your teeth closer to their ideal positions.

5

Braces Removal & Polishing

Once teeth have moved to their final positions, brackets are carefully removed and teeth are polished to reveal your new, beautifully aligned smile.

6

Retainer Fitting & Aftercare

Custom retainers are provided to maintain your results. Your dentist advises on wear schedule and schedules follow-up visits to ensure lasting alignment.

Care & Safety

Aftercare & What to Expect

😣

Initial Soreness

Mild soreness and pressure on teeth for 2–4 days after fitting or adjustments is normal. Over-the-counter pain relief and soft foods help manage discomfort during this settling period.

🥣

Food to Avoid

Avoid hard, sticky, crunchy, or chewy foods (like nuts, hard candy, popcorn, and chewing gum) that can damage brackets or wires. Soft foods are your best friend during treatment.

🪥

Oral Hygiene is Critical

Brush after every meal using a soft-bristle brush and fluoride toothpaste. Floss daily using a floss threader or interdental brushes to clean around brackets and wires thoroughly.

📅

Wear Your Retainer

After braces are removed, wear your retainer as instructed — typically full-time initially, then nightly. Skipping retainer wear allows teeth to shift back to their original positions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Orthodontic treatment can begin at any age, but it is most efficient between ages 10–14 when jaw growth is still active. However, adults of all ages can achieve excellent results with braces or clear aligners. There is no upper age limit for orthodontic treatment.

Treatment duration depends on the complexity of your case. Minor corrections may take 6–12 months, while moderate to complex cases typically require 18–24 months. Your orthodontist will give you an estimated timeline at your initial consultation after assessing your specific needs.

Braces are not painful to fit, but mild soreness and pressure on teeth is common for 2–4 days after fitting and after each adjustment appointment. This is a sign that the teeth are moving and is entirely normal. Over-the-counter pain relievers and soft foods manage this discomfort easily.

Metal braces are the most affordable and effective for complex cases. Ceramic braces use tooth-coloured brackets that are less visible but slightly more expensive. Clear aligners are removable, nearly invisible trays that are ideal for mild to moderate corrections and offer the most discreet appearance. Your dentist will recommend the best option based on your clinical needs and lifestyle.

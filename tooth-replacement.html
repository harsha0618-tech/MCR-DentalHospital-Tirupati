Overview

What Is Tooth Replacement?

🦷

Dentures

Removable prosthetic teeth designed to replace multiple or all missing teeth. Modern dentures are lightweight, natural-looking, and custom-fitted for comfort and a secure bite.

🌉

Dental Bridges

A fixed restoration that fills the gap left by one or more missing teeth by anchoring an artificial tooth to the healthy teeth on either side, restoring function and appearance.

🔩

Implant-Supported Restorations

Titanium implants are placed into the jawbone to act as artificial roots, supporting crowns, bridges, or dentures for the most stable and long-lasting tooth replacement available.

✨

Partial Dentures

When only some teeth are missing, a partial denture fills in the gaps while clasping onto remaining natural teeth — a cost-effective and removable solution.

Candidacy

Who Needs Tooth Replacement?

🦷

Missing One or More Teeth

Whether you have lost a tooth due to decay, trauma, or extraction, replacement prevents neighbouring teeth from drifting and the jawbone from shrinking.

😔

Low Confidence in Your Smile

Gaps in your smile can affect self-esteem in social and professional situations. Tooth replacement restores a full, natural-looking smile.

🍽️

Difficulty Eating

Missing teeth make it hard to chew properly, limiting your diet and affecting nutrition. Replacement restores full chewing function and comfort.

🗣️

Speech Difficulties

Gaps caused by missing front teeth can cause lisping or slurring. Properly fitted replacements restore clear, confident speech.

🦴

Bone Loss Prevention

The jawbone needs tooth roots for stimulation. Implant-supported replacements prevent bone resorption that causes facial sagging over time.

⚠️

Shifting of Remaining Teeth

A gap from a missing tooth causes neighbouring and opposing teeth to drift or over-erupt. Replacement keeps your remaining teeth properly aligned.

Step by Step

The Tooth Replacement Process at MCR

1

Comprehensive Examination

Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy evaluates your teeth, gums, and jawbone health using X-rays and clinical examination to determine the most suitable replacement option for you.

2

Treatment Planning

A personalised treatment plan is created based on the number of missing teeth, bone density, budget, and your preference for fixed or removable solutions.

3

Preparation (If Required)

For bridges, adjacent teeth are gently prepared. For implants, the titanium post is placed into the jawbone under local anaesthesia in a minor surgical procedure.

4

Impressions & Custom Fabrication

Precise impressions of your mouth are taken and sent to our dental lab, where your crowns, bridge, or denture are custom-crafted to match your natural teeth.

5

Fitting & Placement

Your replacement tooth or prosthetic is carefully fitted and adjusted to ensure a comfortable bite, perfect fit, and natural appearance before final placement.

6

Aftercare & Follow-up

You receive tailored care instructions and scheduled follow-up visits to monitor healing and ensure your replacement remains comfortable and functional long-term.

Care & Safety

Aftercare & What to Expect

🥣

Soft Diet Initially

After placement, stick to soft foods for the first few days to allow your gums and bone to settle comfortably around the new restoration without undue pressure.

🪥

Maintain Oral Hygiene

Brush twice daily and floss around bridges and implants using interdental brushes or floss threaders. Remove and clean dentures nightly in a soaking solution.

🚫

Avoid Hard & Sticky Foods

Hard foods like ice or hard sweets and sticky foods like caramel can crack crowns or dislodge bridges and dentures. Avoid these to extend the life of your restoration.

📅

Regular Dental Check-ups

Visit MCR every 6 months so we can professionally clean your restoration, check fit and integrity, and identify any issues before they become problems.

The procedures are performed under local anaesthesia so you will not feel pain during treatment. After implant placement you may experience mild soreness for a few days, which is easily managed with prescribed pain relief. Denture fittings and bridge placements are generally very comfortable with minimal post-procedure discomfort.

Dentures and bridges can typically be completed in 2–4 appointments over 2–3 weeks. Dental implants require a longer timeline — around 3–6 months — to allow the implant to fuse with the jawbone (osseointegration) before the final crown is placed. Your treatment timeline will be discussed clearly at your consultation.

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime — the titanium post is permanent, though the crown may need replacement after 15–20 years. Dental bridges typically last 10–15 years. Dentures generally require relining or replacement every 5–8 years as the jawbone naturally changes shape over time.

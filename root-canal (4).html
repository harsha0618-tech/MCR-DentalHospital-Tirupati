Overview

What Is Root Canal Treatment?

🦷

The Problem

When bacteria reach the inner pulp of your tooth through deep decay or cracks, it causes intense pain and infection that won't heal on its own.

🔬

Pulp Removal

The infected or inflamed pulp tissue is carefully removed from the root canals using precision instruments, eliminating the source of pain and infection.

🧹

Cleaning & Shaping

Each canal is thoroughly cleaned, disinfected, and shaped to remove all bacteria, ensuring no infection remains inside the tooth.

🛡️

Sealed & Restored

The canals are sealed with a biocompatible material and the tooth is restored with a crown — strong, functional, and virtually indistinguishable from natural.

Warning Signs

Signs You May Need a Root Canal

😣

Severe Toothache

Persistent, throbbing pain — especially when biting, chewing, or applying any pressure to the tooth — is a classic warning sign.

🌡️

Prolonged Sensitivity

Lingering sensitivity to hot or cold foods and drinks that doesn't go away even minutes after the stimulus is removed.

🟤

Tooth Discolouration

A tooth that has turned dark grey or brown may indicate the internal pulp has died and begun to decay from within.

🫧

Gum Swelling or Pimple

Swollen, tender gums or a recurring pimple-like bump near a tooth are signs of an abscess caused by infection spreading to the root tip.

🔓

Cracked or Chipped Tooth

A crack or chip that reaches the pulp exposes it to bacteria. If left untreated, this quickly leads to infection requiring RCT.

🪨

Deep Decay

Cavities that have been neglected for too long eventually reach the pulp. Once bacteria invade the root, a filling alone cannot fix it.

Why Choose RCT

Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

✅

Saves Your Natural Tooth

Nothing replaces a natural tooth. RCT lets you keep your own tooth for decades — preserving your jaw structure and bite alignment.

😌

Immediate Pain Relief

Contrary to its reputation, root canal treatment relieves pain — not causes it. Most patients feel significantly better after the very first appointment.

🦠

Stops the Infection

Without treatment, a tooth infection can spread to the jaw, neck, and beyond. RCT eliminates the infection at its source, protecting your overall health.

💰

Cost-Effective Long Term

Saving a tooth with RCT is far more affordable in the long run than extraction followed by an implant or bridge to fill the gap.

🍽️

Normal Chewing Restored

After restoration with a crown, the treated tooth functions exactly like any other — eat your favourite foods without hesitation.

😊

Natural Appearance

A well-matched crown placed after RCT is virtually invisible. Your smile looks completely natural and unaffected.

Step by Step

The RCT Process at MCR

1

Diagnosis & X-Ray

Dr. Ramamurthy Reddy evaluates your symptoms, takes digital X-rays to assess the extent of infection, and confirms whether root canal treatment is needed.

2

Local Anaesthesia

The area around the tooth is numbed completely with local anaesthesia. You remain comfortable and pain-free throughout the entire procedure.

3

Accessing the Pulp

A small opening is made in the top of the tooth to gain access to the infected pulp chamber and root canals beneath.

4

Pulp Removal & Cleaning

Using specialised instruments, the infected pulp is removed, and each canal is carefully cleaned, shaped, and disinfected to eliminate all bacteria.

5

Canal Filling & Sealing

The cleaned canals are filled with a biocompatible rubber-like material called gutta-percha and sealed tightly to prevent any future infection.

6

Crown Placement

A custom-made dental crown is placed over the treated tooth to restore its full strength, function, and natural appearance. Your tooth is as good as new!

Care & Recovery

Aftercare & What to Expect

💊

Mild Discomfort

Some sensitivity or mild soreness for 2–3 days after treatment is normal and is easily managed with over-the-counter pain relievers prescribed by your dentist.

🥣

Soft Diet Initially

Avoid chewing on the treated side until your permanent crown is placed. Stick to soft foods and avoid very hot or very cold foods for a few days.

🪥

Oral Hygiene

Continue to brush and floss normally. Good oral hygiene is the most important factor in the long-term success of your root canal treatment.

📅

Follow-Up Visits

A follow-up appointment is scheduled to fit your permanent crown. Thereafter, routine 6-monthly check-ups ensure the tooth remains healthy and strong.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Modern root canal treatment is performed under effective local anaesthesia, so the procedure itself is no more uncomfortable than getting a routine filling. Most patients are pleasantly surprised by how painless it is. The treatment actually relieves the severe toothache you were already experiencing.

At MCR, most root canals are completed in 1–2 sittings, depending on the severity of the infection and the complexity of the tooth's root anatomy. Molars with multiple canals may require 2 visits, while front teeth are often completed in a single appointment.

Saving your natural tooth is almost always the better choice. After extraction, the gap must be filled with an implant or bridge — both of which are more expensive and complex than RCT. Natural teeth also maintain jawbone density and alignment in a way that replacements cannot fully replicate.

With a properly placed crown and good oral hygiene, a root canal-treated tooth can last the rest of your lifetime. Success rates for root canal treatment are very high — over 95% when performed correctly and restored with a quality crown.

The cost varies depending on which tooth is being treated (front teeth have fewer canals than molars) and whether a crown is needed. At MCR, we offer transparent, affordable pricing with no hidden charges. Call or visit us for a personalised quote after a brief examination.

Delaying treatment allows the infection to spread further into the jawbone and surrounding teeth. This can lead to a dental abscess, bone loss, or the tooth becoming unsalvageable — requiring extraction. Early treatment gives the best outcome and is always the most cost-effective choice.
